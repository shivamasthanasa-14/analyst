About

Background

I am an MBA graduate from the University of Mannheim with experience across consulting, programme operations, digital workflows, and data-led decision support. My work has involved translating complex operational problems into structured models, dashboards, requirements, and management-ready recommendations.

This portfolio brings that way of working into selected business cases. The focus is not on volume, but on showing how I think: clarifying the problem, identifying what needs to be measured, separating symptoms from root causes, and turning analysis into practical next steps.

I am currently based in Germany and open to roles where business analysis, data interpretation, process improvement, and stakeholder communication sit close together.

What this shows

Each case is designed to show the complete analytical path: from an unclear business question to a structured issue tree, a working model, key findings, and a recommendation. It is meant to make my thinking visible, not just display final charts.

How I work

Frame the decision

I start by clarifying the business question, the decision to be made, the constraints, and the stakeholders who need the answer. This keeps the work anchored in action, not just analysis.

Build the logic

I break the problem into drivers, assumptions, KPIs, process steps, and root causes. The goal is to create a model that is simple enough to explain and strong enough to support a recommendation.

Make it usable

I turn findings into clear outputs: a short storyline, decision options, trade-offs, and next steps. The final deliverable should help a stakeholder move forward with confidence.
